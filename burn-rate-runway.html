How to Manage Startup Runway in 2026

Startup runway is the number of months your business can survive before running out of cash. In the current economic climate, understanding your Net Burn Rate is vital for timing your next fundraise or strategic pivot.

The Calculation Example

To calculate runway, you divide your total cash balance by your Net Monthly Burn Rate. For example: If you have $500,000 in the bank, monthly expenses of $50,000, and monthly revenue of $10,000:

  • Net Burn: $50,000 (Expenses) - $10,000 (Revenue) = $40,000
  • Runway: $500,000 / $40,000 = 12.5 Months

Survival FAQs

What is the difference between Gross Burn and Net Burn?

Gross Burn is the total amount of cash leaving your bank account each month (Rent + Salaries + AWS). Net Burn is your actual loss (Gross Burn minus Revenue). Runway is always calculated using Net Burn.

How can I extend my runway without raising money?

The fastest ways to extend runway are reducing non-essential headcount, renegotiating software contracts, and focusing on high-margin customer segments to increase revenue immediately.

What does "Default Alive" mean?

Coined by Paul Graham, you are "Default Alive" if your current trajectory (growth and expenses) leads you to profitability before you run out of cash. Use our Breakeven Tool to verify this.
