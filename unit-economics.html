Understanding Unit Economics

Unit economics are the direct revenues and costs associated with a particular business model, and expressed on a per-unit basis. A "unit" might be a single customer (in SaaS) or a physical item sold (in E-commerce).

Contribution Margin vs. Gross Margin

Founders often confuse these two, but in 2026, investors look closely at the distinction:

  • Gross Margin: Revenue minus COGS (The cost of the product itself).
  • Contribution Margin: Gross Margin minus all other variable costs (like shipping, credit card fees, and direct ad spend). This tells you how much money is actually left to pay for your rent, payroll, and fixed overhead.

The Importance of "Breakeven Units"

Our calculator includes a Break-even Volume projection. This represents the number of units you must sell every month just to reach $0 in net profit. If your contribution margin is low, you need massive volume to survive.

The SaaS Benchmarks

For high-valuation SaaS companies, the gold standard is a **70-80% Contribution Margin**. This allows for the high R&D and sales spending required to capture a market. If your margin is below 40%, you may be operating a "services" business disguised as software.
