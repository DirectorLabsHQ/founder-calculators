Understanding ROAS in 2026

ROAS stands for Return on Ad Spend. It is a marketing metric that measures the amount of revenue your business earns for each dollar it spends on advertising.

ROAS vs. ROI: What's the Difference?

While they sound similar, they serve different purposes for a founder:

  • ROAS: Only looks at revenue vs. ad spend. It’s a "top-of-funnel" health check.
  • ROI (Return on Investment): Looks at the bottom-line profit after accounting for COGS, shipping, and agency fees.

The Breakeven ROAS Formula

Knowing your breakeven ROAS is the most important part of scaling ads. If your ROAS drops below this number, you are losing money on every sale.

Breakeven ROAS = 1 / Gross Margin %

Example: If your margins are 50%, your breakeven ROAS is 2.0x. If your margins are 80% (common in SaaS), your breakeven is 1.25x.

"High ROAS can hide a dying business."

In 2026, with privacy-first tracking (iOS 17+ and beyond), reported ROAS in ad managers like Meta or Google is often an estimate. Always cross-reference your MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio): Total Revenue / Total Marketing Spend.
