How to Interpret LTV:CAC Without Lying to Yourself

LTV:CAC is the golden ratio of SaaS — until founders start gaming it. Here's how to use it honestly.

Built by people who’ve had to defend this number to investors (and themselves).

What LTV:CAC is supposed to tell you

Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost ratio answers one question: Is every dollar you spend on acquiring customers worth more than a dollar in return?

The classic rule: aim for 3:1 or higher. Below 1:1 = you’re destroying value. Between 1:1 and 3:1 = you’re treading water.

The four ways founders lie with LTV:CAC (and how to catch them)

  1. Inflating LTV with fantasy retention
    Most founders use historical average lifetime (e.g., 36 months) even when current churn is spiking. If churn doubled last quarter, your real LTV is half what you think.

    Fix: Use cohort-based LTV or forward-looking retention curves.

  2. Using gross revenue instead of gross profit
    LTV should be gross profit (revenue minus COGS), not total revenue. If your gross margin is 60%, your real LTV is 60% of what you’re claiming.

    Fix: Always multiply revenue LTV by gross margin %.

  3. Ignoring blended vs paid CAC
    If 40% of your customers come from referrals or organic, your real CAC is lower than your ad spend suggests. Using only paid CAC makes your ratio look worse than it is.

    Fix: Calculate blended CAC (total marketing spend / total new customers).

  4. Comparing apples to oranges across stages
    A 3:1 ratio at $5k MRR is very different from 3:1 at $500k MRR. Early-stage businesses can survive lower ratios because growth compounds faster.

    Fix: Adjust expectations by stage (see benchmarks below).

Realistic LTV:CAC Benchmarks (2026)

Pre-$50k MRR (Early Stage)

  • Good: 2:1 – 3:1
  • Acceptable: 1.5:1 – 2:1
  • Danger: <1.5:1

You can survive lower ratios here because growth compounds faster at this stage.

$50k–$250k MRR (Scaling)

  • Good: 3:1 – 4:1
  • Acceptable: 2.5:1 – 3:1
  • Danger: <2.5:1

Investors start demanding efficiency. Below 3:1 raises red flags.

$250k+ MRR / $1M+ ARR (Mature Growth)

  • Good: 4:1+
  • Acceptable: 3:1 – 4:1
  • Danger: <3:1

At this scale, low ratios kill profitability and make fundraising harder.

A quick note on payback period

A great LTV:CAC with a 24-month payback can still kill cash flow — always cross-check with your actual runway and burn rate.
