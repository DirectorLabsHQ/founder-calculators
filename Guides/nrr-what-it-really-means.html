Net Revenue Retention (NRR): The Metric That Predicts Survival

If you only track one number long-term, make it NRR. It tells you whether your business is secretly dying — even when MRR looks good.

Written by operators who’ve watched NRR <100% quietly kill companies (and >120% quietly make them unstoppable).

What NRR actually measures

Net Revenue Retention (NRR), sometimes called Net Dollar Retention, answers one question: From the same customers you had 12 months ago, how much revenue do you have today — after churn, downgrades, and expansion?

NRR = (Starting MRR from cohort + Expansion – Churn – Downgrades) ÷ Starting MRR from cohort × 100%

NRR > 100% = expansion > churn → net negative churn (the holy grail). NRR = 100% = you’re flat (no net growth from existing customers). NRR < 100% = you’re losing revenue from your base — every month.

Why NRR is more predictive than MRR or growth rate

MRR can look beautiful while your business quietly decays. NRR exposes the truth:

  • It measures real product stickiness
    High NRR means customers love the product enough to spend more over time. Low NRR means they’re leaving or shrinking — no amount of new logos can fix that forever.
  • It predicts long-term capital efficiency
    NRR >110% means every dollar of CAC compounds faster because existing customers keep paying (and paying more). NRR <90% means you’re in a treadmill — running faster just to stay in place.
  • Investors obsess over it
    In 2026, NRR is often the first number VCs ask for after MRR and gross margin. Below 100% for multiple quarters? Red flag. Above 120%? Green light for aggressive scaling.

Realistic NRR Benchmarks (2026)

Pre-$50k MRR (Early Stage)

  • Strong: **100–110%**
  • Healthy: **90–100%**
  • Caution: **80–90%**
  • Danger: **<80%**

Early stage, NRR near 100% is fine — focus first on product-market fit before worrying about expansion.

$50k–$250k MRR (Scaling)

  • Strong: **110–130%+**
  • Healthy: **100–110%**
  • Caution: **90–100%**
  • Danger: **<90%**

Below 100% at this stage is a serious warning — expansion must outpace churn or growth stalls.

$250k+ MRR / $1M+ ARR (Mature Growth)

  • Strong: **120–140%+**
  • Healthy: **110–120%**
  • Caution: **100–110%**
  • Danger: **<100%**

At this scale, NRR <110% often means repricing, product pivots, or customer success overhauls.

A quick note on churn interaction

Long payback is especially dangerous when churn is high, because customers may leave before you ever recover CAC. Always cross-check payback with your churn rate and net retention.
