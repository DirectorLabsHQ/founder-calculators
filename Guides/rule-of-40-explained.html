Rule of 40 Explained: When Growth Hides Problems

The Rule of 40 remains the single most quoted SaaS health check — but most founders misunderstand what it actually signals.

Built by operators who’ve had to explain this number to boards and investors.

What the Rule of 40 actually is

The Rule of 40 says: a SaaS company is healthy if its annual revenue growth rate (%) + profit margin (%) ≥ 40.

Example: 50% YoY growth + –5% profit margin = 45 → passes. 20% growth + 30% profit margin = 50 → passes. 80% growth + –50% margin = 30 → fails.

It’s not a law of physics — it’s a heuristic that balances growth vs sustainability. VCs love it because it’s simple and correlates with long-term outcomes.

The three big ways founders misread the Rule of 40

  1. Chasing growth at any cost
    A 100% growth rate with –70% margins looks “healthy” on paper (30 > 40? No — 100 – 70 = 30 < 40). But if burn is unsustainable, the Rule of 40 is lying to you.

    Reality check: High growth only excuses negative margins when growth can still compound quickly and runway is long.

  2. Ignoring the quality of growth
    40% growth driven by one-time deals or heavy discounts inflates the number but destroys future revenue. The Rule doesn’t see cohort decay or bad unit economics.

    Fix: Always look at net revenue retention alongside Rule of 40.

  3. Using it too early or too late
    Pre-$1M ARR: Rule of 40 is less relevant than retention and product-market fit. Post-$10M ARR: If you're below 40 consistently, investors start asking hard questions about path to profitability.

Realistic Rule of 40 Benchmarks (2026)

Pre-$1M ARR (Early / Seed Stage)

  • Strong: >40 (often 60–100+ from pure growth)
  • Healthy: 30–40
  • Caution: 20–30
  • Danger: <20

Negative margins are normal here — investors care more about retention and product-market fit than profitability.

$1M–$10M ARR (Series A/B)

  • Strong: >50
  • Healthy: 40–50
  • Caution: 30–40
  • Danger: <30

Below 40 consistently starts raising questions about path to profitability. Expansion must start kicking in.

$10M+ ARR (Growth / Late Stage)

  • Strong: >60
  • Healthy: 40–60
  • Caution: 30–40
  • Danger: <30 (especially if margins are still negative)

At this scale, staying below 40 for multiple quarters often means repricing, cost cuts, or restructuring.

A quick note on churn interaction

Remember: Rule of 40 doesn’t see churn — a high score can hide deteriorating customer retention. Always cross-check with your net revenue retention (NRR) and churn benchmarks.
