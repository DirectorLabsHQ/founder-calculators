The SaaS "Leaky Bucket" Phenomenon

Projecting SaaS revenue isn't just about how many new customers you can acquire. It's about the relationship between your Monthly Growth Rate and your Gross Churn.

What is a Good Growth Rate?

In 2026, venture capital expectations have shifted toward sustainable growth. However, the "T2D3" (Triple, Triple, Double, Double, Double) path still dictates the milestones for high-growth startups:

  • Early Stage: 15-20% month-over-month growth.
  • Growth Stage: 100% year-over-year growth.
  • Mature SaaS: 30-40% year-over-year growth (Rule of 40).

Why Churn is the Silent Killer

A 5% monthly churn rate might seem small, but it means you lose 46% of your customers every year. To simply stay flat, you have to replace half your business annually. This makes "Negative Churn" (where expansion revenue from existing customers outweighs lost revenue) the holy grail of SaaS unit economics.

Strategic Insight: ARR vs. MRR

While MRR helps you manage day-to-day operations, Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) is the metric used for valuation. Most SaaS companies in 2026 are valued at a multiple of their year-end ARR.
